Coffee shop sales

Project Overview

The objective of this project was to analyze coffee shop sales data, create meaningful insights, and build an interactive dashboard using Tableau. The dashboard allows for dynamic exploration based on location of sales trends by month, day of the week, hour of the day, and product performance.

Dataset Description

    • Transaction details (ID, date, time, quantity)
    • Store information (ID, location)
    • Product metrics (ID, price, category, type, details)

Dataset Preparation

    To prepare the data for analysis, the following steps were taken:
    1. Explored the dataset to understand the number of transactions and the range of recorded dates.
    2. Identified different product categories and types.
    3. Added a calculated field Sales by calculating unit Price × Quantity.

Data Analysis

  • Sales trend analysis
  • Product performance evaluation
  • Store-level comparisons
  • Time-based pattern recognition

Tools and Technologies

    • Tableau – Data visualization & dashboard creation.
    • Excel – Data exploration & preprocessing.

Key Findings & Impact

There is a steady growth in sales from January to June,June recorded the highest revenue at $166.49K, showing an increasing trend.
  • More Revenue is recorded for coffee sales and least revenue recorded for Packaged chocolate
  • Monday is the busiest day with $102k sales followed by wednesday and friday compared to weekends which has marked lowest sales with an average of $97k
  • Most sold Item Flavours has less profitable compared to less sold Coffee Beans is more Profitable
  • Location-based insights
  • Revenue optimization opportunities

    • Business Recommendations

      The top five revenue-generating products are:
      1. Coffee - $269.95K (leading contributor)
      2. Tea - $196.41K
      3. Bakery - $82.32K
      4. Drinking Chocolate - $72.42K
      5. Coffee Beans - $40.09K
      These items significantly drive sales, with coffee and tea being the standout performers.
      Conversely, the bottom products by sales are:
      1. Branded Items - $13.61K
      2. Loose Tea - $11.21K
      3. Flavours - $8.41K
      4. Packaged Chocolate - $4.41K (lowest performer)
      These low-performing items may benefit from targeted promotions or strategic adjustments during peak hours,Product mix improvements and Location-specific marketing initiatives to improve their sales performance.
